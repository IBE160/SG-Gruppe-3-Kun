## Executive Summary

The chatbot provides HMSREG users (suppliers, subcontractors, construction workers, project managers, and administrators) with quick and accurate answers to questions about using HMSREG, requirements, procedures, documentation, and troubleshooting. The goal is to reduce support workload, ensure consistent answers, improve user efficiency, and make HMSREG documentation more accessible.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Users of the HMSREG system, including suppliers, subcontractors, construction workers, project managers, and administrators, frequently encounter challenges in efficiently accessing critical information. The extensive and detailed nature of HMSREG documentation, while comprehensive, makes it time-consuming to navigate and locate specific details regarding documentation requirements, certificates, HMS cards, and system procedures. This difficulty leads to:

-   **Inefficiency**: Users spend valuable time searching for answers, diverting focus from their primary tasks.
-   **Frustration**: The inability to quickly find information can cause user dissatisfaction and delays in critical processes.
-   **Increased Support Workload**: Support personnel are often burdened with repetitive queries that could be resolved by more accessible documentation.
-   **Potential for Errors**: Misinterpretation or oversight of documentation due to navigation difficulties can lead to compliance issues or operational mistakes.

The core problem is the friction between the need for quick, accurate information and the time-consuming process of navigating complex documentation, impacting user efficiency and increasing support demands.

### Problem Impact

The current challenges in accessing HMSREG documentation have several significant business impacts:

-   **Increased Support Workload**: A substantial portion of support inquiries are dedicated to answering routine questions that could be self-served if documentation were more accessible. This diverts valuable support resources from addressing more complex issues.
-   **Reduced User Efficiency**: Users, including critical on-site personnel like construction workers and project managers, experience delays and inefficiencies when they cannot quickly find answers. This directly impacts productivity and project timelines.
-   **Inconsistent Information**: Without a centralized, easily queryable source, there's a risk of inconsistent answers being provided by different support channels or through varied interpretations of documentation.
-   **Hindered Adoption/Compliance**: Difficulty in understanding and adhering to HMSREG procedures due to inaccessible documentation can slow down system adoption and potentially lead to non-compliance with regulations.
-   **Opportunity Cost**: Time spent by users and support staff on documentation navigation is time not spent on core business activities, representing a significant opportunity cost.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

The primary existing "solution" for accessing HMSREG information is manual navigation through extensive documentation, likely in the form of user guides, FAQs, and procedural manuals. This approach falls short in several key areas:

-   **Time-Consuming Search**: Users must actively search, read, and interpret large volumes of text to find specific answers, which is inefficient, especially for urgent on-site queries.
-   **Lack of Personalization**: The current documentation is generic, failing to provide tailored answers based on a user's specific role (e.g., a construction worker's needs differ significantly from a project manager's).
-   **Static and Passive**: Documentation is static, requiring users to know what they are looking for and how to phrase their search queries effectively. It does not proactively assist or clarify ambiguities.
-   **Limited Accessibility**: While documentation may be online, its format might not be optimized for quick mobile access or for users who prefer conversational interfaces.
-   **No Contextual Troubleshooting**: Existing documentation provides general information but lacks the interactive troubleshooting capabilities needed to guide users through specific problems they might encounter (e.g., a failed check-in).
-   **Absence of Feedback Loop**: There's no direct, integrated mechanism for users to provide immediate feedback on the helpfulness of documentation or to flag areas for improvement, leading to potential staleness or unaddressed pain points.

These gaps highlight the need for a more dynamic, interactive, and user-centric approach to documentation access.

### Proposed Solution

The core vision is to develop an "HMSREG Documentation Chatbot" that acts as an intelligent assistant for various HMSREG users. This chatbot will provide quick, accurate, and role-based answers to questions regarding HMSREG documentation, requirements, procedures, and troubleshooting.

Key aspects of this initial vision include:
-   **Role-based personalization**: Tailoring answers based on the user's role (e.g., Worker, Supplier, Project Manager).
-   **Interactive Chat Interface**: A user-friendly interface for asking questions.
-   **Comprehensive Knowledge Base**: Covering topics like workforce registration, documentation requirements, certificates, HMS cards, and check-in/out procedures.
-   **Accurate Information Retrieval**: Answering questions based on official HMSREG documentation from docs.hmsreg.com.
-   **Automatic Fallback**: Guiding users to support channels or direct documentation links when an answer cannot be found.
-   **Search and Retrieval**: Efficiently finding relevant information within the extensive HMSREG documentation.

This solution aims to significantly reduce the support workload, ensure consistency in information dissemination, and enhance user efficiency by making critical documentation easily accessible.

### Key Differentiators

The HMSREG Documentation Chatbot differentiates itself from existing static documentation and generic search tools through several key features:

-   **Role-Based Personalization**: Unlike one-size-fits-all documentation, the chatbot will tailor responses based on the user's specific role (e.g., Construction Worker, Supplier, Project Manager). This ensures that information is not only accurate but also highly relevant to the user's context and needs.
-   **Interactive and Conversational Interface**: Moving beyond keyword searches, the chatbot offers a natural language interface, allowing users to ask questions conversationally and receive direct, concise answers, significantly reducing the time and effort required to find information.
-   **Proactive Assistance (Future Extension)**: The vision includes intelligently offering help when a user is detected to be struggling on a documentation page, transforming passive documentation into an active support system.
-   **Interactive Troubleshooting (Future Extension)**: The chatbot aims to guide users through troubleshooting steps based on their specific situation, providing a dynamic problem-solving experience rather than just static instructions.
-   **Context-Aware Responses**: The ability to ask clarifying questions and understand user intent allows for more precise and helpful responses, avoiding generic answers that may not fully address the user's underlying query.
-   **Centralized and Consistent Knowledge**: By drawing answers exclusively from official HMSREG documentation, the chatbot ensures consistency and accuracy across all responses, eliminating discrepancies that might arise from varied interpretations.
-   **Reduced Support Burden**: By empowering users to self-serve answers to common questions, the chatbot directly reduces the volume of routine inquiries to support staff, allowing them to focus on more complex issues.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

The primary users of the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot are **Construction Workers**.

**Their Situation:** Construction workers are often on-site, needing quick, on-the-go access to information regarding their documentation requirements, such as HMS cards, check-in/out procedures, and personal certifications. They frequently encounter issues like failed check-ins ("HMS-kort ugyldig") and require immediate, clear guidance to resolve them and continue their work. They are typically mobile-first users who need concise, actionable answers without navigating complex manuals.

**What they are doing today without the solution:** They currently face frustration and delays when check-ins fail or when they need to verify documentation. They might resort to asking colleagues, calling support, or attempting to navigate dense online documentation on a mobile device, all of which are time-consuming and inefficient.

**What would make them say 'finally, someone gets it!'?:** A chatbot that instantly provides step-by-step troubleshooting for common issues like HMS card validity, guides them through check-in procedures, and confirms their documentation status in a simple, conversational manner, directly on their mobile device. They would appreciate role-specific answers that cut through irrelevant information.

### Secondary Users

Beyond the primary construction workers, several other user types will significantly benefit from the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot:

-   **Suppliers and Subcontractors**:
    -   **Their Situation**: These users are responsible for registering their workforce, managing certificates, and ensuring all HMS cards are valid and compliant. They frequently need to understand detailed documentation requirements for various projects and personnel.
    -   **What they are doing today without the solution**: They spend considerable time sifting through regulations and guides to ensure accurate and timely registration, often contacting support for clarification on specific compliance points.
    -   **What would make them say 'finally, someone gets it!'?:** A chatbot that provides instant, accurate answers on workforce registration procedures, certificate upload requirements, and HMS card management, helping them avoid penalties and streamline their administrative tasks.

-   **Project Managers and Construction Project Owners**:
    -   **Their Situation**: These stakeholders require a high-level overview of project status, deviations, and comprehensive documentation to ensure compliance and smooth project execution. They need quick access to information that impacts project oversight and decision-making.
    -   **What they are doing today without the solution**: They may struggle to quickly retrieve specific compliance details or verify documentation status across multiple sub-contractors, relying on manual reports or direct inquiries.
    -   **What would make them say 'finally, someone gets it!'?:** A chatbot that can rapidly summarize documentation requirements for specific project phases, clarify compliance standards, and provide insights into common deviation issues, enabling faster decision-making and risk mitigation.

-   **Administrative and Support Personnel**:
    -   **Their Situation**: This group handles a wide range of user inquiries and is responsible for training new users. They need a reliable, consistent source of information to provide accurate support and reduce their workload.
    -   **What they are doing today without the solution**: They often spend time searching through documentation themselves or escalating queries, leading to longer resolution times and potential inconsistencies in answers.
    -   **What would make them say 'finally, someone gets it!'?:** A chatbot that serves as a first-line support tool, providing consistent answers to frequently asked questions, thereby freeing up their time to focus on more complex or unique support cases. It also acts as a valuable training resource.

### User Journey

The user journey for the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot is designed to be intuitive and personalized, starting with a critical role selection step to tailor the subsequent interaction.

**Initial Interaction - Role Selection:**
1.  **Access**: A user initiates interaction with the chatbot (e.g., by opening the chatbot interface on a mobile device or web portal).
2.  **Welcome & Prompt**: The chatbot greets the user with a welcome message and immediately prompts them to select their role from a predefined list (e.g., "Construction Worker," "Supplier / Subcontractor," "Project Manager / Admin"). This is crucial for contextualizing future responses.
3.  **Role Confirmation & Context Setting**: Upon selection, the chatbot confirms the chosen role and explicitly states that all answers will be based *only* on official documentation from `docs.hmsreg.com` to manage expectations regarding information accuracy and scope.
4.  **Session Context**: The selected role is stored in the session context, ensuring that all subsequent interactions and answers are filtered and presented from that specific user perspective.

**Subsequent Interaction - Information Retrieval:**
Following role selection, the user proceeds to ask questions related to HMSREG documentation. The chatbot's internal process, as detailed in the `proposal.md`'s "User Flow" diagram, involves:
-   **Query Submission**: User types a question into the chat interface.
-   **Backend Processing**: The query is sent to the backend, where it undergoes rate limiting, vector similarity search against the HMSREG documentation knowledge base, and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) pipeline processing.
-   **Gemini 2.5 Pro Generation**: An AI model generates a context-aware response in Norwegian (or English, if detected), leveraging the retrieved documentation chunks and the user's role.
-   **Response Display**: The chatbot presents the answer to the user, potentially including clarifying questions or links to relevant documentation.
-   **Feedback Loop**: Users can provide feedback (e.g., "helpful" or "not helpful") on the chatbot's responses, contributing to continuous improvement.

This journey is exemplified by scenarios such as a construction worker troubleshooting a failed check-in, where the chatbot guides them through the process of understanding and resolving issues like an invalid HMS card. The flow is designed to be efficient, providing immediate, relevant support and reducing the need for manual documentation searches or direct support contact.

## Success Metrics

Success for the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot will be defined and measured across several key areas, focusing on accuracy, user satisfaction, operational efficiency, and system performance.

The primary metrics that will demonstrate the chatbot's effectiveness and impact are:

-   **Accuracy and Helpfulness**: The chatbot must provide accurate and helpful answers to at least **80%** of all HMSREG documentation questions it receives. This indicates the core effectiveness of its RAG capabilities.
-   **User Satisfaction**: A direct measure of user experience, aiming for a consistent user satisfaction rating of **4/5 or higher**. This will be collected via in-chat feedback mechanisms (e.g., thumbs up/down).
-   **Reduced Support Workload**: A measurable **reduction in support ticket volume** specifically related to common HMSREG documentation questions. This quantifies the chatbot's impact on operational efficiency.
-   **Response Time**: The chatbot must maintain an average response time of **under 5 seconds** for standard queries, ensuring a fluid and responsive user experience.
-   **Effective Fallback**: The chatbot must successfully identify and escalate questions it cannot confidently answer, providing appropriate fallback options (e.g., redirecting to support or direct documentation links). This ensures reliability and manages user expectations.
-   **Documentation Improvement**: Analytics data generated by the chatbot will be utilized to identify documentation gaps and frequently asked topics. This insights will directly contribute to improving existing HMSREG resources and content.

### Business Objectives

The deployment of the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot is strategically aligned with several key business objectives:

-   **Optimize Operational Efficiency**: By automating responses to common documentation queries, the chatbot aims to significantly reduce the workload on human support staff, allowing them to focus on more complex and high-value issues.
-   **Enhance User Productivity**: Empowering HMSREG users with instant access to accurate information will minimize downtime spent searching for answers, thereby improving their overall productivity and efficiency in their daily tasks.
-   **Improve Information Consistency and Quality**: The chatbot will serve as a single, authoritative source of information derived directly from official HMSREG documentation, ensuring that all users receive consistent and up-to-date guidance.
-   **Drive Continuous Documentation Improvement**: Through the collection and analysis of user queries and feedback, the chatbot will provide invaluable insights into documentation gaps and areas of frequent confusion, directly informing and prioritizing future documentation updates and enhancements.
-   **Increase User Satisfaction and Adoption**: A more accessible and responsive information retrieval system is expected to lead to higher user satisfaction with the HMSREG platform and foster greater adherence to its procedures.

### Key Performance Indicators

To track progress towards the stated business objectives and overall success, the following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) will be monitored:

-   **Chatbot Accuracy Rate**: Measured as the percentage of user questions for which the chatbot provides a correct and complete answer. **Target: ≥80%**.
-   **User Satisfaction Score**: Derived from in-chat user feedback (e.g., thumbs up/down ratings). **Target: Average score of 4/5 or higher**.
-   **Support Ticket Volume Reduction**: Percentage decrease in support tickets related to common documentation queries post-chatbot deployment. **Target: To be defined post-baseline analysis, but aiming for a significant reduction (e.g., 15-25%)**.
-   **Average Response Time**: The mean time taken for the chatbot to generate and deliver a response to a user query. **Target: Under 5 seconds**.
-   **Fallback Activation Rate**: The frequency with which the chatbot correctly identifies questions it cannot answer and triggers its fallback mechanism. This indicates the system's robustness and ability to manage expectations.
-   **Documentation Gap Identification**: Number of unique documentation gaps or frequently asked, unresolved topics identified through chatbot analytics, leading to subsequent documentation updates.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

The Minimum Viable Product (MVP) of the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot will focus on delivering essential functionalities that directly address the core problem of inaccessible documentation and provide immediate value to users. The MVP will include:

-   **Role-Based Personalization**: At the start of each session, the chatbot will prompt the user to select their role (e.g., Worker, Supplier, Project Manager). This selection will enable the chatbot to provide tailored and contextually relevant answers, ensuring a personalized experience from the outset.
-   **Intuitive Chat Interface**: A user-friendly chat interface will allow users to naturally ask questions related to HMSREG documentation. This interface will be designed for ease of use across various devices, including mobile.
-   **Comprehensive Knowledge Base**: The chatbot will be powered by a robust knowledge base encompassing critical HMSREG topics, including workforce registration, documentation requirements, certificates, HMS cards, and check-in/out procedures.
-   **Official Documentation as Source**: All answers provided by the chatbot will be strictly based on official HMSREG documentation sourced from `docs.hmsreg.com`, ensuring accuracy and reliability.
-   **Automatic Fallback Mechanism**: In instances where the chatbot cannot confidently answer a query, it will gracefully fall back by providing users with clear guidance to alternative support channels or direct links to relevant sections of the official documentation.
-   **Efficient Search and Retrieval**: The chatbot will incorporate advanced search and retrieval capabilities to quickly identify and present the most relevant information from the HMSREG documentation in response to user queries.

These core features represent the smallest set of functionalities required to validate the hypothesis that a conversational AI can significantly improve access to HMSREG documentation and reduce support burden.

### Out of Scope for MVP

To maintain focus and ensure a timely delivery of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP), the following functionalities, while valuable, are considered out of scope for the initial release:

-   **Proactive Assistance**: The MVP will not include features that intelligently detect user struggle on documentation pages or proactively offer help.
-   **Interactive Troubleshooting**: Complex, multi-step interactive troubleshooting guides based on specific user situations will not be part of the initial release.
-   **Advanced Clarifying Questions**: While role-based personalization is in scope, advanced conversational capabilities to ask clarifying questions beyond initial role selection to understand nuanced user context will be deferred.
-   **Detailed Log Analysis for Documentation Improvement**: While basic analytics will be collected, in-depth analysis and automated identification of documentation improvement areas from conversation logs will not be a primary MVP feature.
-   **Enhanced UI/UX beyond core functionality**: Significant UI/UX improvements and advanced design elements beyond a functional chat interface will be considered for future iterations.
-   **Multi-language support (beyond Norwegian/English)**: While the system will handle Norwegian and English queries, support for additional languages is out of scope for the MVP.
-   **Integration with HMSREG login credentials**: The MVP will focus on public documentation access and will not require HMSREG login credentials.

### MVP Success Criteria

The success of the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) will be measured against the following criteria, ensuring that the core functionalities deliver tangible value:

-   **Core Accuracy**: The chatbot must accurately answer at least **80%** of questions related to the defined MVP scope (e.g., workforce registration, HMS cards, check-in/out procedures) based on official documentation.
-   **User Acceptance**: Achieve a user satisfaction rating of **4/5 or higher** from early adopters interacting with the MVP.
-   **Support Load Reduction**: Demonstrate a measurable decrease in the volume of support inquiries directly related to the MVP's covered topics.
-   **Performance**: Maintain an average response time of **under 5 seconds** for all queries within the MVP's scope.
-   **Reliable Fallback**: The MVP must consistently and appropriately trigger its fallback mechanism when presented with questions outside its knowledge base or confidence threshold, guiding users to alternative support.
-   **Role-Based Relevance**: Successfully deliver contextually relevant answers based on the user's selected role, as evidenced by user feedback.

### Future Vision

While the MVP focuses on core functionality, the long-term vision for the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot includes several advanced features and enhancements that will further elevate the user experience and utility:

-   **Proactive Assistance**: Implementing intelligent mechanisms to detect when a user might be struggling on a documentation page (e.g., based on time spent, erratic scrolling, or repeated searches) and proactively offering relevant help or suggestions.
-   **Example Scenarios / "What do I do if..." Guides**: Developing interactive guides that walk users through common scenarios and provide step-by-step instructions for specific situations.
-   **Interactive Troubleshooting**: Enhancing the chatbot's ability to engage in dynamic, context-aware troubleshooting based on a user's specific problem, guiding them towards a resolution.
-   **Advanced Clarifying Questions**: Improving the chatbot's natural language understanding to ask more nuanced clarifying questions, ensuring a deeper understanding of user intent and providing even more precise responses.
-   **Log of Common Questions for Analysis**: Expanding the logging and analytics capabilities to provide more granular insights into frequently asked questions, unresolved queries, and documentation gaps, directly feeding into continuous improvement cycles.
-   **Multi-Language Support Expansion**: While Norwegian and English are covered, future iterations could include support for additional languages relevant to the diverse workforce in the construction industry.
-   **Improved UI with Enhanced User Experience**: Continuous refinement of the user interface to provide a more seamless, intuitive, and visually appealing experience.
-   **Deeper Integration**: Exploring integrations with other HMSREG modules or external systems to provide a more holistic support experience.

## Technical Preferences

The HMSREG Documentation Chatbot will be built upon a modern, robust, and scalable technical architecture, prioritizing open-source technologies, cloud-native deployment, and efficient AI integration.

### System Architecture Overview

The system comprises a user-facing frontend, a Python-based backend, and a combination of vector and relational databases, all integrated with Google AI services.

-   **Frontend**: Developed with **Next.js 14 (App Router) and TypeScript**, utilizing **Tailwind CSS** and **shadcn/ui** for a responsive and accessible chat interface. Static documentation pages will be pre-rendered using SSG.
-   **Backend**: Implemented in **Python 3.11+** using **FastAPI**, chosen for its high performance, asynchronous capabilities, and automatic OpenAPI documentation. It will host the core API endpoints and the LangChain RAG pipeline.
-   **Vector Database**: **ChromaDB** will be used for storing and retrieving document embeddings, offering a free, open-source, and persistent solution for semantic search.
-   **Relational Database**: **PostgreSQL on Supabase** will manage structured data, including conversations, feedback, rate limits, and analytics, leveraging its free tier and built-in features.
-   **AI Integration**: **Google AI API** will be the primary provider for AI services, specifically **Gemini 2.5 Pro** for chat responses and **text-embedding-004** for generating document embeddings. These models are selected for their multilingual support (including Norwegian), advanced reasoning, and cost-effectiveness.

### Key Technical Choices and Justification

-   **Next.js 14 + TypeScript**: Provides a robust framework for both dynamic chat UI and static documentation, ensuring type safety and performance.
-   **FastAPI**: Ideal for AI/ML integration due to its speed and ease of use, facilitating rapid development of the backend API.
-   **LangChain**: A standard framework for RAG applications, simplifying the integration of document retrieval and LLM generation.
-   **ChromaDB**: A practical choice for a project of this scale, offering efficient vector storage and retrieval without significant infrastructure overhead.
-   **Google Gemini 2.5 Pro**: Offers state-of-the-art multilingual capabilities, crucial for Norwegian language support, and a generous free tier for production use.
-   **Supabase**: A managed PostgreSQL service with a free tier that provides excellent integration with Next.js and Python, along with essential database features.

### Deployment and Hosting

-   **Frontend Hosting**: **Vercel** (Free tier) for automatic deployments and fast global access.
-   **Backend Hosting**: **Railway** (Free tier) for Python + FastAPI deployment, offering automatic HTTPS and environment variable management.
-   **Database Hosting**: ChromaDB will be deployed with the backend, and PostgreSQL will be hosted on Supabase.

This architecture ensures a scalable, maintainable, and cost-efficient solution capable of delivering a high-quality user experience.

## Organizational Context

The development of the HMSREG Documentation Chatbot will be managed through a structured, iterative approach, emphasizing clear milestones, continuous testing, and a strong feedback loop to ensure alignment with organizational goals and user needs.

### Development Timeline and Milestones

A 6-week development plan is proposed, broken down into weekly milestones:

-   **Week 1: Environment Setup and Data Collection**: Focus on setting up the development environment, collecting and processing HMSREG documentation, and initializing version control.
-   **Week 2: Backend API and RAG Pipeline**: Develop the core Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline and implement the basic FastAPI backend.
-   **Week 3: Frontend Chat Interface and Integration**: Build the responsive chat UI and integrate it with the backend API.
-   **Week 4: Testing, Refinement, and Mobile Responsiveness**: Conduct comprehensive testing, refine the RAG pipeline for accuracy, and ensure mobile responsiveness.
-   **Week 5: Fallback Handling, Feedback System, and Analytics**: Implement robust fallback mechanisms, integrate a user feedback system, and set up analytics tracking.
-   **Week 6: Final Testing, Deployment, and Documentation**: Perform final quality assurance, deploy the chatbot to production, and prepare comprehensive project documentation.

### Testing Strategy and Evaluation

A rigorous testing strategy will be employed to ensure the chatbot meets its success criteria:

-   **Test Question Set Development**: A comprehensive set of 50-100 representative test questions will be created, covering all user stories and documentation topics, with diversity in language, complexity, and phrasing.
-   **Evaluation Metrics**: Primary metrics include Accuracy Rate (target ≥80%), Relevance Score, Completeness Score, Response Time (target <5 seconds), and Fallback Accuracy. Secondary metrics will cover Source Citation Quality, Norwegian Language Quality, and Consistency.
-   **Testing Methodology**: A combination of automated testing (batch evaluation) and manual evaluation (human review by two independent reviewers) will be used.
-   **Iterative Refinement**: Test results will drive iterative improvements in the RAG pipeline, prompt engineering, and documentation coverage.
-   **User Testing (Optional but Recommended)**: Recruitment of 3-5 users from target groups for task-based scenarios and free exploration, followed by feedback surveys and debrief interviews to gather qualitative insights.
-   **Feedback Loop and Continuous Improvement**: Post-deployment, continuous monitoring of user feedback, analysis of unresolved questions, and regular documentation refreshes will ensure ongoing improvement.

### Risk Management

Key risks, such as documentation scraping complexity, RAG accuracy, Norwegian language quality, API cost overruns, and deployment challenges, have been identified with corresponding mitigation strategies. The project will prioritize MVP features, test continuously, and utilize AI-assisted coding to accelerate development.

This structured approach ensures that the project is well-managed, risks are mitigated, and the final product is thoroughly validated against its objectives.
